The Gurkha Army Regiment

Fearless warriors from the hills of Nepal

History

The Gurkha Army Regiment traces its origins back to the early 19th century when British East India Company soldiers recognized the bravery and skill of Nepalese warriors. Known for their loyalty and fierce combat tactics, Gurkhas have served in various armies including the British and Indian armies.

Traditions

Gurkhas are known for their distinctive kukri knives, which are both a weapon and a symbol of pride. Their motto, “Kayar Hunu Bhanda Marnu Ramro” means "Better to die than live a coward". This warrior spirit is reflected in their discipline and customs.

Famous Battles

Gurkhas have fought bravely in many conflicts including both World Wars, the Falklands War, and various peacekeeping missions. Their resilience and skill have earned them many medals and respect worldwide.

Modern Day Gurkhas

Today, Gurkhas continue to serve in elite units and special forces. Their legacy lives on as symbols of courage and honor in modern military forces around the world.
